## 2. List Building Strategy

### Website Signup Points

**1. Footer Signup** ✅
- Persistent on every page
- Email field + "Subscribe" button

**2. Homepage Section** (Consider adding)
- Value prop: "Get early access to new collections + exclusive offers"

**3. Exit-Intent Popup** (Future)
- Triggers when cursor moves toward browser close
- Offer: 10% off first purchase OR free shipping

**4. Checkout Thank You Page** (Future)
- "Join our collector list for first access to limited editions"

### Signup Incentive Options

| Incentive | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|------|------|
| 10% off first order | High conversion | Cuts into margin |
| Free shipping | High perceived value | Cost absorbed |
| Early access to new work | No cost, builds exclusivity | Lower conversion |

**Recommendation:** "Early access to limited editions + 10% off your first purchase"

## 3. Email Sequences (To Implement)

### 3.1 Welcome Sequence

**Email 1: Immediate**
- Thank them for joining
- Deliver discount code (if offered)
- Brief intro: Who you are, what you create
- One stunning image
- CTA: Browse the collection

**Email 2: Day 2**
- Your story (fisherman → photographer → artist)
- Why you photograph the Hamptons
- 2-3 images showing range
- CTA: Explore collections

**Email 3: Day 4**
- Quality explanation (archival materials, etc.)
- Behind-the-scenes of production
- CTA: See available sizes

**Email 4: Day 7**
- Reminder of discount code (if unused)
- Bestsellers or featured pieces
- Urgency: Code expires soon
- CTA: Shop now

### 3.2 Abandoned Cart Sequence

**Email 1: 1 hour after**
- Image of abandoned product
- "You left something beautiful behind"
- Simple CTA: Complete your purchase

**Email 2: 24 hours after**
- Address common objections (shipping, guarantee)
- CTA: Return to cart

**Email 3: 72 hours after**
- Final reminder with discount code
- CTA: Complete purchase

### 3.3 Post-Purchase Sequence

**Email 1: Immediate** ✅ (Order confirmation exists)
- Order details
- Production timeline
- What to expect

**Email 2: Day 3**
- Behind-the-scenes of printing process
- Build anticipation

**Email 3: Shipping**
- Tracking number
- Expected delivery

**Email 4: Day 30**
- Check in on satisfaction
- Ask for photo of artwork installed
- Request review/testimonial

## 4. Ongoing Email Calendar

### Frequency
- **Minimum:** 2 emails per month
- **Recommended:** 3-4 emails per month

### Content Types

**New Work / Collection Spotlight**
- 3-5 new images
- Story behind the shoot
- CTA: View collection

**Behind-the-Scenes / Story**
- Personal narrative
- Process insights
- Connection building (no hard sell)

**Social Proof / Featured Piece**
- Customer testimonial or installation photo
- Featured piece with story
- CTA: Shop similar pieces

**Promotion (Occasional)**
- Clear offer with deadline
- Urgency/scarcity
- Strong CTA

### Seasonal Ideas

| Season | Content Ideas |
|--------|---------------|
| Spring | Fresh starts, new work |
| Summer | Beach content, peak season |
| Fall | "Bring summer home," autumn colors |
| Winter | Gift guides, holiday shipping deadlines |

## 5. Email Design Guidelines

### Visual Style
- Single column, mobile-first
- 600px max width
- White background
- High quality images (<200KB each)

### Structure
```
[Logo]
[Hero Image - Full Width]
[Headline - 6-10 words]
[Body Copy - 2-3 short paragraphs]
[CTA Button]
[Footer: Social links, unsubscribe, address]
```

### Best Practices
- Subject lines: 40 characters or less
- Use preview text
- Always include alt text on images
- One primary CTA per email
- Test on mobile
